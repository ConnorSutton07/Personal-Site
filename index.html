Personal Statement

I am a 4th year undergraduate student at the University of Kansas working towards a B.S. in Computer Science and a B.G.S. in Anthropology in addition to a minor in mathematics.

During my time as an undergraduate, I have tried to explore as many interesting applications of Computer Science as possible, including Game Design, Artifical Intelligence, Natural Language Processing, Computational Anthropology, and Biodiversity Informatics. The common thread that runs through all of my projects is their aim; from using cellular automata and Bayesian modelling to understand important factors in Pleistocene hominin dispersal, to applying sentiment analysis and other NLP techniques to finding trends in translation of ancient Greek literature, I am always aiming for a deeper understanding of humanity's past. It is my objective to use my training in computational methods and anthropology to explore social organization, human cognition, and the development of civilizations.
